What We Do

Research at the Quantum–Biology Interface

Our interdisciplinary lab investigates how quantum phenomena shape neuronal function, and how those insights can lead to new diagnostics and therapies for neuroinflammatory illness.

Light in Quantum Neurobiology

Investigating how light interacts with neuronal structures, studying optical properties of key proteins to reveal quantum mechanisms underlying brain function.

Learn more →

Magnetics in Quantum Neurobiology

Studying radical pair spin dynamics and how magnetic fields and isotope effects influence cognitive processes and neuronal activity at the quantum level.

Learn more →

Chemicals in Quantum Neurobiology

Exploring how anesthetics, psychedelics, and nerve agents relate to neuroinflammation and neurodegeneration at the quantum molecular level.
